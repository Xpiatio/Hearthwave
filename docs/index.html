An assistive bridge for GMRS

No one should be left off the channel.

Voice radio has always excluded people who can't hear it or can't speak into it. Hearthwave closes that gap — every transmission becomes readable text, and every typed message can go out over the air in a natural voice.

  • Deaf & hard of hearing

    Read every transmission live — incoming voice is captioned automatically as it arrives.

  • Speech & non-speaking

    Type a message and have it synthesized and broadcast over the air in your station voice.

  • Built-in access

    Read Aloud mode, large-touch targets, full keyboard navigation, and WCAG 2.2 AA throughout.

One GMRS license covers the whole household. Each person gets their own account, voice, and preferences — kids on tablets, parents on laptops, grandparents in large-touch mode. Everyone on the same channel, no app download required.

Assign listen-only accounts to greeters and ushers and TX-capable accounts to the safety lead; the NCS roster tracks who is on station, and SKYWARN alerts auto-announce when severe weather approaches.

For accessible communication

Hearthwave turns GMRS into a two-way text bridge. Members who are deaf or hard of hearing read every transmission live; members who can't speak type a message and have it synthesized and broadcast over the air — automatically tagged with the station callsign. No one is left off the channel.

On the panel

Everything your household needs.

Built for real families and watch groups — from first-time operators to licensed net control stations.

  • Live transcription

    Incoming voice hits text in real time via Whisper — partials appear while the operator is still talking. A saved-phrases list lets admins prime Whisper with group-specific vocabulary.

  • Two-tier accuracy

    A fast model streams live partials while an optional larger model re-transcribes each finished transmission in full, replacing the on-screen text with a sharper, gap-free final.

  • Cuts through static

    Adaptive squelch tracks the channel noise floor so weak carriers don't clip the first word, and TX conditioning keeps your synthesized voice clear over narrowband FM.

  • Multi-user profiles

    Each family member has their own login, avatar, voice, and preferences. No shared account juggling.

  • Voice PTT

    Speak into your browser mic. The server transcribes your voice, synthesizes it with Piper TTS, then broadcasts it over the air — callsign included.

  • Senior-friendly design

    WCAG 2.2 AA, large touch targets, Read Aloud mode, and plain-language status messages — no jargon.

  • SKYWARN weather alerts

    National Weather Service alerts for your county push to every connected device and auto-announce over the air.

  • Session journals

    Save and publish transcripts from each radio session. Optional AI summaries powered by Gemini.

  • FCC callsign lookup

    Verify GMRS and amateur callsigns against the FCC database. Confirmed contacts show a green check. Family members sharing one callsign are stored separately and addressed by name.

  • One-command install

    Docker Compose on any Linux server. No database to manage — flat-file storage keeps it simple and crash-resistant.

  • Keyboard PTT

    Hold Space to transmit from any keyboard. A pre-roll buffer captures the first syllable before you press — never lose the start of your message.

  • Priority audio mixer

    Six traffic priority levels — Routine through Emergency — with AGC and low-pass filtering. Higher-priority transmissions pre-empt lower ones.

Licensed & legal

Staying legal with GMRS.

Hearthwave is designed from the ground up for FCC Part 95 compliance — here's what that means in plain language.

Get your GMRS license

GMRS requires an FCC license, but there's no exam. A single $35 application covers your entire immediate family. Your callsign arrives within a few days and is valid for 10 years.

Apply at FCC.gov →

Automatic station identification

The FCC requires you to identify your station. Hearthwave handles this without you lifting a finger. Your callsign is spoken at the end of every transmission, and a standalone ID announcement goes out every 15 minutes during active use. It can't be turned off.

Remote control, not an internet gateway

The FCC prohibits using GMRS as an internet gateway — bridging audio between two separate radios over the internet. Hearthwave doesn't do that. The radio stays at your home; you're simply controlling it remotely, the same way you'd control any home device from your phone. This is a remote control point, and it's explicitly permitted.

Only licensed family members transmit

Your GMRS license covers your immediate family. Hearthwave enforces transmit access through password-protected accounts — you decide which family members can key up. Non-licensed members can have a listen-only account. After three failed login attempts, the account locks automatically.

Modules

Extend it for your net.

Hearthwave ships with two plugins built in. More can be added without touching the core server.

Net Control Station

BUILT-IN

Full-featured NCS panel for managing radio nets — roster, emergency interrupt, and audio replay.

  • ✦
    Live roster — Check operators in and out with traffic priority levels, Routine through Emergency.
  • ✦
    BREAK BREAK interrupt — Drains the TX queue and broadcasts an emergency interrupt to clear the channel instantly.
  • ✦
    15-second audio replay — Replay the last 15 seconds of received audio when you need to catch a missed transmission.
  • ✦
    Auto net ID — Periodic station announcements at a configurable interval — FCC-compliant by default.

SKYWARN Alerts

BUILT-IN

Monitors your NWS county zone every 5 minutes and pushes severe weather alerts to the whole household.

  • ✦
    Red alert banner — Active NWS warnings appear at the top of every connected browser immediately.
  • ✦
    Browser notifications — Opt-in push notifications reach family members on background tabs or locked screens.
  • ✦
    Auto-announce over the air — When the channel is clear, alerts are automatically read aloud over GMRS via TTS.
  • ✦
    Per-county targeting — Set your NWS zone code once in config — only alerts relevant to your area come through.

Build your own plugin

Bring it on air

Up and running in minutes.

Hearthwave runs anywhere Docker runs — any Linux server on your home network. No cloud accounts, no monthly fees.

Quick install

# 1. Clone the repo
git clone https://github.com/Xpiatio/Hearthwave
cd Hearthwave

# 2. Run setup (creates .env)
./setup.sh

# 3. Start the stack
docker compose up -d

# 4. Open in your browser
http://your-server-ip

What you need

  • x86 mini PC or NUC running Linux
    Intel N100 minimum; N305 or better for the two-tier final pass. 8 GB RAM minimum, 16 GB recommended, Docker installed. Whisper STT needs a real x86 CPU.
  • Audio cable to the radio
    A K1 (or equivalent) cable from the radio's 3.5 mm combo jack — into the computer's built-in jack, or a USB sound card. No USB-to-radio connection required.
  • VOX, or a USB PTT dongle
    VOX keying needs no wiring (a primer tone opens the radio first). Or key PTT via a USB serial adapter (RTS/DTR) or a CM108 sound-card GPIO.
  • GMRS radio + license
    A Part 95-certified GMRS radio and an FCC GMRS license (covers your whole family).
  • Local network
    Family members connect from any device on your home Wi-Fi — no port forwarding needed.
